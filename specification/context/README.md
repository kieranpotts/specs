# Context

This part describes the problem space the system operates in — the descriptive background a reader needs before the [requirements](../requirements/) make sense. Where requirements are prescriptive (what the system must do), the context is descriptive: what the domain _is_, who participates in it, and the conditions the system must work within.

Nothing here is a requirement in its own right. These artifacts are the shared frame of reference from which the requirements are derived.

## Sections

- [**Overview**](./overview/): A high-level description of the system — its mission, the problem it solves, and its scope.

- [**Constraints**](./constraints/): The conditions imposed on the system from outside — regulatory, legal, and business constraints, standing assumptions, and external dependencies.

- [**Glossary**](./glossary/): The project's ubiquitous language — every domain term, briefly defined.

- [**Model**](./model/): The entities in the system's domain model, and how they relate to one another.

- [**Actors**](./actors/): The participants in the domain — people, organizations, and external systems — and the hierarchy between them.
